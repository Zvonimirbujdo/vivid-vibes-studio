Important Do's and Dont's after getting a Tattoo

Follow Your Tattoo Artist's Aftercare Instructions:

Keep the tattoo clean by gently washing it with lukewarm water and mild, fragrance-free soap. Pat it dry with a clean towel.

Apply a thin layer of the recommended aftercare ointment or unscented lotion to keep the area moisturized.

Protect Your Tattoo from Sun Exposure:

Keep your tattoo out of direct sunlight for at least 2-3 weeks. Sunlight can fade the colors and prolong healing.

Once your tattoo is fully healed, apply sunscreen to protect it from UV damage and preserve its vibrancy.

Wear Loose, Breathable Clothing:

Opt for loose clothing that doesn’t rub against your tattoo. Tight clothing can cause irritation or trap moisture, slowing down the healing process.

Don’t Pick or Scratch the Tattoo:

As your tattoo heals, it will scab and might feel itchy, but picking or scratching can lead to scarring, infection, or damage to the design.

Don’t Submerge Your Tattoo in Water:

Avoid swimming pools, hot tubs, or long baths for at least 2-3 weeks. Excessive moisture can cause the tattoo to soften, increasing the risk of infection and ink loss.

Don’t Over-Moisturize:

While keeping the tattoo moisturized is important, applying too much ointment can suffocate the skin, trapping bacteria and slowing down the healing process. Apply a thin, even layer as directed.
